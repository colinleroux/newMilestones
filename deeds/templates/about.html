About deeds

A calmer way to get started and keep going.

deeds is built around a simple idea: goals feel lighter when they are visible, practical, and broken down into steps you can actually do. Whether your aim is tiny or long-term, the app helps you focus on the next useful action.

The rhythm

  1. 1. Create a goal that feels worth moving toward.
  2. 2. Add simple steps until the goal stops feeling vague.
  3. 3. Tick off progress and let momentum do some of the work.

1. Add a goal

Head to Goal Manager and create something you want to make progress on. It can be as small as "walk three times this week" or as broad as "prepare for a new role".

2. Break it into steps

Use the weekly planner to add steps to that goal. Keep them concrete and manageable. If a step still feels heavy, it probably needs to be made smaller.

3. Check off progress

Mark steps as completed when you do them. That visible progress is the point: it creates evidence that you are moving, even when the bigger goal still takes time.

A simple way to use the app

  • Start small. Pick one goal you can make clearer today.
  • Add only the next few steps. You do not need the entire plan upfront.
  • Use past dates if needed. If you forgot to log something, you can still add it afterward.
  • Review often. The dashboard helps you see what is active this week and what is already done.
  • Archive finished goals. When all steps are complete, move the goal into your completed list and keep the record of what you achieved.

Good first goal ideas

Personal

Stretch every morning, journal three times this week, tidy one room.

Work

Finish a proposal draft, update your portfolio, prepare for a meeting.

Health

Walk after lunch, prep two simple meals, book a check-up.

Learning

Read one chapter, complete one lesson, practice for twenty minutes.
