Quick Guide

A simple workflow for using deeds

Use this as a gentle path through the app: capture ideas, turn them into goals, work through steps, and keep records only when they help you.

1. Start with a contemplated item

Use the brain button when something is still forming. Add ideas, possibilities, or priorities there first so nothing important gets forgotten.

2. Promote the right idea into a goal

Once something feels ready, convert it into a goal or create a fresh goal directly. Goals are for the things you want to actively move forward.

3. Add steps that feel achievable

Break the goal into small steps in the step planner. Keep them light and practical so they feel doable rather than daunting.

4. Check in daily and tick things off

Use the dashboard to see what is scheduled for today, this week, or this month. Mark steps complete as you go and add more when life changes.

5. Keep records if you want them

Reflections and logs are optional. Use reflections to capture how a step felt, and logs when you want activity details like time, distance, sets, reps, or notes.

6. Revive or reshape goals when needed

Completed goals move into the achieved list. If you want to add more steps later, simply mark the goal as not achieved and carry on.
